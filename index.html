Sant Cugat, Barcelona
  • €2,990/2wk (~€1,495/wk); €4,890/4wk (~€1,223/wk). Full board, 18 sessions/2wk, Camp Nou + PortAventura, transfers, laundry. (2026 sold out.)
Marcet Mid
Barcelona · Fundación Marcet
  • ~$1,350/wk (youngest/Development tier via reseller). 30–35 hrs training, full staff, nutritionist menus. Tiered pricing rises with age/level.
IMG Academy & Nike (US) Premium / benchmark
Florida (IMG) · US universities (Nike)
  • IMG ~$4,349–$4,829/wk boarding (day ~$3,849+) — the US premium anchor. Nike/US Sports Camps ~$449–$919/wk overnight — the US mass-market benchmark.
Read: the pro-club "foundation" camps sell brand experience at mid prices; IMG/Barça-Barcelona sell premium venue/facility. IDA's real head-to-head is the independent HP operators (SIA, Kaptiva, Marcet) — where IDA prices above SIA/Kaptiva but bundles more and adds a placement pathway.

What the numbers say

  • IDA is priced at the top of the mid band (~$1,785). Above Real Madrid Foundation (€1,250), SIA (€995), AC Milan and Kaptiva; ~level with Real Madrid MAX (€1,750); below Barça-Barcelona and IMG.
  • SIA undercuts you by ~$800/wk (€995). A residential, full-board, 16-session camp for under €1,000 is the value threat — your bundle and pathway have to justify the gap.
  • Pro-club "foundation" camps are cheaper than their brand suggests (Real Madrid €1,250, AC Milan €1,350) because they're mass-participation experiences — you compete on real development, not nostalgia.
  • You already publish your price (Prep Camps) — an advantage over the quote-only residency market; extend that clarity to the other camp lines.

Pricing / go-to-market moves

  • Justify the premium over SIA/Kaptiva — lead with the all-inclusive bundle (kit, transfers, excursions, language) + placement pathway + US↔Europe choice.
  • Add a day-camp or entry tier (like Real Madrid's €395–€625) to capture price-sensitive/local families and feed the funnel.
  • Use camps as the top of the funnel into IDA's residency academy and placement services — the camp is a paid trial.
  • Anchor against Barça-Barcelona/IMG — "elite development + real pathway at less than half the flagship price."

Strategic Takeaways

Where IDA's camps stand and how to grow the line.

Positioning in one line

  • IDA camps are a top-of-mid-band, all-inclusive, pathway-linked product — priced above the pro-club foundation camps and independents like SIA, but below the Barça-Barcelona/IMG premium. The pitch is development + placement, not brand nostalgia.

Where you win / can press

  • Real pathway, not a brand day-out. Foundation camps (Real Madrid, AC Milan) sell an experience; IDA sells development + a college/pro route. Make that the headline.
  • All-inclusive clarity. Your $1,785 already bundles kit, meals, transfers, excursions, language — market the "no hidden extras" value vs. cheaper base prices that add on.
  • US + Europe optionality (Valencia + Daytona) — few competitors offer both sides of the Atlantic.
  • Camp → academy funnel. Use camps as a paid trial that converts into residency + placement revenue.

Risks / gaps to close

  • SIA's €995 undercut — a full-board, high-session camp for less than €1,000 pressures your price on value-shoppers.
  • Brand halo of Barça/Real Madrid/AC Milan pulls families on name at similar or lower prices.
  • No published entry/day tier — you cede the price-sensitive and local-family segment.
  • Only Prep Camps publish pricing — the elite lines are invisible until a sales conversation.

Recommended next actions

  • Sharpen the value story vs. SIA/Kaptiva — quantify what's bundled so the ~$800 premium over SIA is obvious and defensible.
  • Launch a day-camp / entry tier to compete with Real Madrid's €395–€625 and widen the funnel.
  • Engineer the camp→residency→placement funnel with clear upsell offers and conversion tracking.
  • Publish pricing for the elite camp lines (or "from" figures) to enter comparison shortlists.
  • Position against Barça-Barcelona/IMG on value — elite development + pathway at < half the flagship price.
  • Refresh annually before each summer-camp booking cycle.
