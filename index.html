What the Camps Actually Deliver

Beyond price, families are buying a bundle. Here's the deliverable set by dimension — and which camps are strong vs. thin on each. The dividing line is "experience" vs. "development" vs. "development + exposure."

Brand / experience

  • Man City · Nike/Liverpool · Barça-BCN · Real Madrid · AC Milan. You pay for the crest, the elite facility, the stadium tour and a safe residential wrap. Real coaching, but little individual reporting and essentially no recruiting pipeline.

Development / methodology

  • Tovo · SIA · Kaptiva · Marcet. You buy the coaching pedagogy and volume (16–18 sessions/wk, ex-La Masia/UEFA-Pro staff). Strong development; lighter on excursions and formal exposure.

Development + exposure

  • IDA · Eture · FCV. Development plus a pathway — video/reels, matches vs academies, reports, and (IDA/Eture) direct routes to college/pro. This is IDA's lane.

What the numbers say

  • IDA (~$1,785) sits just below the premium tier. The UK club camps (Man City £2,199, Nike/Liverpool £2,295) and Tovo (~$2,150) are ~$1,000/wk more — for the badge/method, not more exposure.
  • SIA still undercuts you by ~$800/wk (€995) and FCV (~£1,250) sits below you too — value pressure from below.
  • On the exposure axis you're premium-justified. Eture (your closest pathway rival) gates its price; you publish yours and bundle more.

Pricing / go-to-market moves

  • Sell the exposure deliverable (reels, reports, placement) to defend the premium over SIA/FCV and justify parity with the brand camps.
  • Add a day-camp / entry tier (vs. Real Madrid €395–625) to widen the funnel and capture price-sensitive families.
  • Contrast with the UK club camps — "real development + a college/pro pathway for ~$1,000/wk less than a crest and a stadium tour."
  • Use camps as the paid trial into IDA's residency academy + placement services.

Strategic Takeaways

Where IDA's camps stand and how to grow the line.

Positioning in one line

  • IDA camps are a top-of-mid-band, all-inclusive, "development + exposure" product — priced below the brand/facility premium (IMG, Barça-BCN, Man City, Nike/Liverpool, Tovo) and above the mid-market field, competing on a real pathway rather than a crest.

Where you win / can press

  • Pathway, not nostalgia. The UK club camps (Man City, Liverpool) and Barça/Real Madrid sell the crest; you sell development + college/pro exposure at ~$1,000/wk less.
  • All-inclusive clarity + published price vs. gated (Eture) or add-on-heavy (club camps) pricing.
  • US + Europe optionality (Valencia + Daytona) — unusual in this field.
  • Camp → academy funnel — a paid trial that converts into residency + placement revenue.

Risks / gaps to close

  • Value pressure from below — SIA (€995) and FCV (~£1,250) undercut you.
  • Brand halo of Man City / Liverpool / Barça / Real Madrid pulls families on name.
  • Eture out-delivers on exposure specifics (Veo reels, NCAA scouts) — you need to match/publicize that.
  • No published day/entry tier and no staff-ratio transparency (nobody publishes one — an easy differentiator).

Recommended next actions

  • Productize the exposure deliverable — Veo-style match reels, individual player reports, and a named college/pro placement track — and put it on the camp page to beat Eture at its own game.
  • Publish a staff-to-player ratio — no competitor does; it's a cheap, concrete trust signal.
  • Sharpen the value story vs. SIA/FCV — quantify the bundle so the premium is obvious.
  • Launch a day-camp / entry tier to widen the funnel (vs. Real Madrid €395–625).
  • Run the "crest vs. pathway" comparison in marketing against Man City/Liverpool.
  • Refresh annually before each summer-camp booking cycle.
